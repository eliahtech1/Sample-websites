Your daily digest of stories shaping online retail

1. Temu Struggles in U.S. Against Amazon’s Pricing Power

Temu’s U.S. user base dropped by 54% between March and mid‑July 2025, after Amazon tightened its pricing dominance. Efforts to pivot toward domestic suppliers and relaunch ads face stiff competition. Sellers caution that Amazon’s control over pricing dynamics remains a major barrier.

2. Walmart Unveils AI “Super Agents” to Boost E‑Commerce

Walmart launched AI‑powered agents—“Sparky,” “Marty,” and more—to personalize shopping, manage orders, and automate internal workflows. With a goal to hit 50% online revenue in five years, these tools aim to help Walmart compete with Amazon on efficiency and experience.

3. Alibaba Integrates Ele.me & Fliggy into E‑Commerce Core

In a strategic realignment, Alibaba merged its food-delivery (Ele.me) and travel (Fliggy) platforms into its main e‑commerce business. The move aims to strengthen its position amid fierce quick‑commerce competition, underscoring demand for integrated offerings.

4. Dark Patterns Advisory Hits Indian E‑Commerce Platforms

India’s Central Consumer Protection Authority has ordered platforms to eliminate misleading UI practices—including fake urgencies, hidden subscription traps, and bait‑and‑switch tactics—to protect consumers and build trust in online retail.

5. AI, AR/VR and Social Commerce Driving 2025 E‑Commerce Trends

AI personalization, immersive AR/VR shopping, voice-activated commerce, and social/live shopping are reshaping the e‑commerce landscape. Sustainability, subscription models, and frictionless payments are also central to winning shoppers this year.
